WaterLab v.1 and Particle Size Distribution

WaterLab v.1 is a device that measures the concentration of specific types of microparticles in water. The particle type is defined or specified by the apparent radius of the microparticle. When measuring, you can focus on a specific radius, e.g, ~1.0 µm, a specified range, or the complete range that the device is able to cover, approximately 0.03~50 µm. The device is capable of "nomincally" measuring up to radii of 100 µm

The WaterLab v.1 device has two versions:

  • WaterLab v1.0 for USB connectivity, for lab or office operation.
  • Waterlab v.1.1, shown in the photo below, 4 ✕ 5.5 ✕ 8 cm3, has an IP-classification nr IP68. It is waterproof when submerged under water for longer periods of time (the highest classification is IP69: can be exposed to high-pressure heated water from various angles), and has a Lora/GSM card for networking.

State-of-the-art water quality parameters

Water quality is quantified by measuring physical, chemical, and biological indicators, often using standardized tests and sensors

  • Physical parameters: measurable characteristics of water that do not change its chemical composition.
    • Temperature: affects dissolved gases and biological activity.
    • Turbidity: measures water clarity; higher turbidity means dirtier water.
    • Color
    • taste and odor
    • Solids
  • Chemical parameters: about chemical properties of the water.
    • pH: acidity or alkalinity, with a neutral pH at 7
    • Dissolved oxygen: crucial for aquatic life; warmer water holds lesse dissolved oxygen
    • Nutrients: such as nitrogen and phosphorus, which can indicate pollution levels.
    • Total dissolved solids(TDS): the concentration of all dissolved substances in water.
    • Chlorine
    • Hardness
    • Biological oxygen demand
  • Biological parameters of water. To asses the presence of living organisms in the water.
    • Bacteria, such as E. coli, a common indicator of fexal contamination.
    • Algae: can impact dissolved oxygen levels and are a sign of nutrient enrichment
    • Viruses
